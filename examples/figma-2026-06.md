# Survival Analysis: Figma

> Application of the **Software Survival Analyzer v4** (Steve Yegge's formula) to Figma, with research evidence as of June 2026. Two scenarios are presented **side by side**: the v4 baseline already recorded in the skill, and an updated version reflecting investment in agent-facing surface area. The human coefficient (H) is decomposed into its two components.

**What it does**: A cloud-native collaborative product-design platform. Combines a real-time multiplayer UI/UX design canvas, design system management, dev handoff (Dev Mode), and since 2025 an AI generation layer (Make, Sites, Buzz, Draw, Weave).

**Type (taxonomy)**: A hybrid of three types. (1) *SaaS for humans* (the collaborative canvas), (2) *Content creation tool* (design/UI generation, increasingly AI-driven), and (3) *Tool for agents* (an emerging layer via the Dev Mode MCP server and Code-to-Canvas). The classification matters: Yegge's model is harsh on types (2) and (3, if poorly executed), while type (1) is protected by H.

---

## Binary Tests

| Test | Result | Justification |
|------|--------|---------------|
| **Absurdity** | **DEPENDS** | Re-synthesizing the real-time multiplayer engine (CRDT/sync, WebGL/C++ render engine, version history, design-tokens infrastructure) would be costly: ~10–12 years of compressed engineering trial-and-error. But generating an individual UI design **is not** absurd: Claude Design, Google Stitch, and v0 already do it directly from a prompt today. The "absurd to re-synthesize" value sits in the collaboration / system-of-record layer, not in design creation. |
| **Intermediation** | **PARTIAL (~40%)** | The part that intermediates between humans and a cognitive task the AI will do on its own is **design generation** (proposing screens, layouts, components), which LLMs increasingly do natively. They do NOT intermediate: multiplayer collaboration, design-system governance, handoff to code, design version control. ~40% of the value sits in the intermediating part, below the 60% threshold, so it isn't treated as a clean YES, but it is a real moderate warning sign. The 2025 product expansion (Sites, Buzz, Make) **raises** this percentage by adding surface that "does smart things LLMs already do natively." |

**Combined read**: Figma cleanly fails neither test, but it doesn't pass either with comfortable margin. This places the base ratio in the middle band (neither indestructible nor threatened) and makes the final forecast depend decisively on H and on strategic trajectory.

### Base ratio calculation

```
Base_ratio = (Savings × Usage) / ((Kc + Fc) × 10)

A (v4 baseline):    (50 × 60) / ((25 + 30) × 10) = 3000 / 550 = 5.5
B (2026 evidence):  (50 × 60) / ((22 + 25) × 10) = 3000 / 470 = 6.4
```

The research raises the base ratio from **5.5 to 6.4**, a +16%, entirely from the reduction in Kc+Fc (awareness and desire paths for agents). Savings and Usage are untouched: the improvement is not "Figma saves more cognition," it is "agents reach Figma with less friction and know it better."

## Human Coefficient (H) decomposition

H measures whether value depends **specifically** on humans being involved. Figma has two H sources with very different half-lives:

| H component | Value | Type (band) | Durability |
|-------------|:---:|-------------|------------|
| **Multiplayer collaboration** (the canvas IS the shared human co-creation space; the team's system of record) | ~85 | Social interaction (80–100) | **Durable.** Slack-like. Protects long-term because people value working together on a shared artifact, not whether AI made it or not. |
| **Design taste** (it's valued that a human did the design, "taste") | ~55 | Creativity/judgment eroding toward trust/oversight (40–60) | **Erodable.** As AI-generated design becomes the norm and is "good enough," the premium for "a human designed it" weakens. This is exactly the band the skill warns erodes over time. |

**Effective H ≈ 72** (boost +1.5 categories). But the strategic conclusion lies in the bifurcation: the **durable floor** of H is collaboration (~85, would give +2), and the **erodable ceiling** is design taste (~55). Figma's long-term protection **does not** rest on people wanting a human to do the design — it rests on the team wanting to collaborate on a shared canvas. This aligns exactly with the observed strategy: retreating to the collaboration / orchestration (system-of-record) layer and giving up the fight on design generation.

## Forecast

| | A · v4 baseline | B · 2026 evidence |
|---|:---:|:---:|
| **Base ratio** | 5.5 | 6.4 |
| **Base category** | 🟠 At risk (border 🟡) | 🟡 Viable |
| **H boost** | +1.5 (H=75) | +1.5 (H=72) |
| **Final forecast** | 🟡 Viable / Protected by H | 🟡→🟢 Protected by H *(conditional)* |
| **Trajectory** | ↓ Worsening | → Bifurcating |

**Synthesis of the two scenarios**: both place Figma in the band **🟡 Viable / 🟢 Protected-by-H**. The research pushes toward the upper edge of that band and improves the trajectory, **but the final forecast is conditional**, not automatic:

- The arithmetic of scenario B (ratio 6.4 + boost +1.5) mechanically gives **🟢 Safe**.
- It is attenuated to **🟡→🟢 conditional** by the Step 7b rule (competition raises the survival threshold) and by the erosion of the design-generation component.
- The condition is clear: Figma reaches 🟢 **if it executes the pivot to orchestration / system-of-record layer** (being the source of truth that agents read from and write to). If that pivot fails and it keeps fighting design generation, it reverts to 🟡 Viable, or to 🟠 At risk in the design component alone.

**Why the trajectory moves from ↓ to →**: the baseline read ↓ because of design-generation erosion. The evidence adds two bullish vectors the baseline didn't capture: (1) strong and growing investment in agent UX (MCP, Code-to-Canvas, MCP-mediated canvas writing) and (2) accelerating business momentum (revenue +46% YoY, record NDR 139%, MCP customers growing seats 70% faster). Net result: **bifurcated** trajectory, orchestration layer rising (↑) and design-generation layer falling (↓), net approximately neutral, clearly better than the flat ↓ before.

## Relative competition (Step 7b)

The survival threshold rises with competition, and Figma is in a now-fierce domain. The analytical key is **where** each competitor attacks:

| Competitor | "Ratio" / nature | Layer attacked | Read |
|------------|------------------|----------------|------|
| **Claude Design** (Anthropic, Apr 2026) | Not a tool the agent "chooses": it's the model doing the design directly. Embodies the test 0b threat (pure disintermediation). | Design generation | The existential wildcard: **Figma's own model provider** shipped a direct competitor. But its collaboration is "basic, not multiplayer" (per research), exactly Figma's moat. |
| **Google Stitch / Stitch 2.0** | Prompt-to-design generator with AI-native canvas. | Design generation | Triggered stock drops (~12% cumulative in March). Attacks the canvas, not the system of record. |
| **v0 (Vercel), Lovable, Bolt** | Prompt-to-code/app. Mid-tier intermediation ratio. | **Canvas bypass** (go straight to code) | The most concrete disintermediation vector: they widen the population that "designs" without opening Figma. Their output still needs refinement and lacks enterprise design-system governance. |

**Competitive insight**: nearly all rivals attack the **commoditizable** layer (design generation), not the **defensible** layer (multiplayer system of record, design-system governance, handoff). Figma's ratio (6.4) is also **higher** than that of pure intermediaries in the same domain (Zapier 3.9, low-code 1.9) because it has more genuine infrastructure and much more H. The question is not whether Figma "wins" design generation (probably not), but whether it retains the orchestration layer while generation commoditizes.

## Desire paths (specific evaluation)

| Question | Answer | Detail |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Full API? | **PARTIAL → growing-YES** | Historical REST API + Plugin API + Dev Mode MCP server (structured context: components, variables, tokens, Code Connect) + write via MCP. The agent surface is already substantial, though creative canvas manipulation via API is still less complete than, e.g., Stripe's API. |
| Monitors failed agent attempts? | **UNKNOWN / emerging** | They clearly iterate on the MCP server based on agent usage (Yegge's exact prescription with Beads), but no public confirmation of formal failure monitoring. |
| Aliases / alternative syntaxes for agents? | **Emerging** | Via MCP (structured tool calls), not at the level of Beads' 100+ subcommands. |
| Error messages informative for agents? | **Improving** | The MCP server is designed for agent consumption. |
| Could an agent without docs guess how to use it? | **Canvas: no. MCP: increasingly yes.** | The canvas is visual (not guessable). The MCP server uses a standard protocol agents already understand. |

**Desire paths verdict**: historically weak, **today the most active investment area**, still maturing. It is the biggest update relative to the v4 baseline, and it is backed by evidence. Yegge's reminder: investing in desire paths has the best ROI of all levers for established software (can drop Fc from 40–50 to 10–15 and double or triple the ratio). Figma is on that path, but it hasn't arrived yet.

## Time horizon

Per Yegge's disruption timeline (2026 = orchestration / multi-agent), Figma's threat sits on the orchestration horizon (~3–4 years). The nuance is that Figma is trying to **be** part of that layer, not be bypassed by it.

- **No changes** (if Figma stood still): ~3–5 years for revenue tied to individual design generation; collaboration lasts longer due to H.
- **With an agent-first strategy** (which Figma is executing, not just considering): the MCP / orchestration pivot extends the horizon significantly. If Figma consolidates as the system of record agents read from and write to, durability approaches Notion/Slack levels (protected by collaboration H), well beyond 5 years.

## Conclusion

Would the agent pass it by, stop to use it, or actively seek it out? **All three, depending on context**, and that is exactly the profile of a 🟡 Viable / 🟢 Protected-by-H in transition:

- An agent designing a UI from scratch **passes by**: generates directly (Claude Design, v0).
- An agent operating **inside a company where Figma is the source of truth** stops to use it: pulls the design system, tokens, and component mappings via MCP.
- And Figma is working to make agents **actively seek it out** as the orchestration layer (Code-to-Canvas, canvas writing).

Figma is **more resilient than its stock price suggests** (down ~75–81% from the post-IPO peak) **and less invulnerable than its growth suggests** (revenue +46%, NDR 139%). Survival as a relevant independent platform is the base case. But its defensibility is **workflow-based, not proprietary-technology-based**: it doesn't own a frontier model, depends on third-party models (including a rival, Anthropic), and is capital-constrained against Adobe (Firefly across tens of millions of seats) and Microsoft (Designer across 400M+ M365 seats).

**Lever-aligned recommendations:**

1. **Lever 5 (desire paths) is the correct bet and must accelerate.** Move the MCP server from beta to de facto standard, instrument failed agent attempts (Beads-style), and implement them as functionality. It is the only axis where Figma can materially and quickly move its ratio.
2. **Lever 6 (H): defend the durable component, not the erodable one.** The long-term moat is multiplayer collaboration (~85), not design taste (~55). The narrative and roadmap should orient toward "system of record for the team and the agents," not toward "the best tool for a human to design."
3. **Product discipline (via checklist, question 6).** The 2025–2026 dispersion (Sites, Buzz, Weave) adds intermediation-exposed surface that doesn't strengthen the moat. The thesis of cutting to the 4 highest-value offerings (Design, Dev Mode, FigJam, Make) is consistent with Yegge.
4. **Monitor the Anthropic relationship as a binary risk.** A change in model access or an escalation of the Claude Design rivalry is the largest discrete threat. Diversification to OpenAI/Google covers part of it.

**Leading indicators to monitor** (thresholds that would change the forecast):
- *NDR sustained ≥130%* = moat intact; drop below ~120% = AI seat erosion.
- *Seats vs. AI credits mix*: if growth migrates from seats to consumption, the per-seat model is being disrupted.
- *MCP / agent adoption*: MCP customers continuing to outpace the rest (70% faster) confirms the orchestration thesis.
- *Gross margin*: stabilization at or above 80% = manageable AI costs.
